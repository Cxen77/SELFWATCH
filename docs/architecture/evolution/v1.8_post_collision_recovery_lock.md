# v1.8: Post-Collision Recovery Lock Phase

Date: 2026-05-23

## Problem
While the `COLLISION_STATE` successfully frozen and protected identities when subjects overlapped, the moment they began separating (exiting the collision group), free association was restored instantly. Because subjects were still physically close and their bounding boxes and appearance features were under transition, the local tracker could aggressively steal or swap identities during the release frames. This caused identity collapse immediately following separation.

## Key Solutions

### 1. Recovery Lock State Propagation
- Inside `memory/occlusion_groups.py`, the `POST_FREEZE_COOLDOWN` window was increased to **25 frames** to establish a robust `RECOVERY_LOCK` state.
- `cooldown_lids` (the set of local IDs currently in post-freeze cooldown) are now extracted in `pipeline.py` and passed down to `tracker.update(...)`.

### 2. Strict Motion Vector & Trajectory Validation
During the `RECOVERY_LOCK` phase, matches must align with historical momentum:
- **Trajectory Consistency**: For moving tracks, we compare the displacement vector of the detection against the track's historical velocity. Matches that diverge (`dot_product < 0.4`) are strictly rejected.

### 3. Tightened Appearance Validation
- We heavily bias previous appearance history: the cosine distance of the match is capped at `0.28` (much stricter than the normal `0.4`). If the visual embedding has changed due to lighting or occlusion, the system prefers to coast the track rather than match a wrong, ambiguous visual.
- The appearance weight (`eff_app_w`) is biased upwards during this lock to prevent loose spatial boxes from pulling the track away from its true identity.

### 4. Cross-Recovery Prevention
We explicitly prevent tracks from the same collision group from swapping IDs:
- **Appearance Comparison**: If a detection looks more like another cooldown track in the group (by a margin of `0.05`), the first track is strictly blocked from matching it.
- **Physical Proximity**: If a detection lies significantly closer (`50px`) to the predicted exit/corridor box of a different cooldown track, matching is rejected.
- **Strict ByteTrack and C-BIoU Gates**: In Stage 2 (`_iou_associate`), the IoU requirements are tightened to `0.6` for cooldown tracks, and C-BIoU fallback matching is completely bypassed.

## Conclusion
By introducing the guided **Post-Collision Recovery Lock** phase, SELFWATCH transitions from a sudden "freeze-to-free-association" model to a guided, physics-backed recovery corridor. Detections are only matched if they perfectly represent the historical trajectory, motion direction, and visual appearance of the separated track, preventing identity switches immediately after subjects exit collisions.
